# Zen Rojas audit — the brand story is real; the conversion infrastructure isn't

> Generated by Qosmic Audit Agent. Crawl performed 2026-06-08.
> zenrojas.com is a live Shopify DTC store; all product, collection, and page data sourced from direct inspection.

## Executive summary

**The Zen Rojas brand has a genuine story and a fragile storefront.** A veteran-owned, family-operated tea company founded mid-deployment during COVID, with 100% organic, Australian-sourced loose leaf blends positioned across Sleep & Relaxation, Immune Support, Daily Energy, and Digestion Support — that is a compelling setup. But 4 of 8 tea SKUs are currently sold out, including the hero products (Premium Sencha, Sleep Tea, Unwind, Heartburn). More than half the catalog is non-purchasable, and none of the sold-out pages capture a back-in-stock email. High-intent visitors who land on a sold-out PDP leave with no action and no way to return. For a small-catalog brand, sold-out products with no re-engagement hook are a direct revenue leak.

**The $50 free-shipping threshold is the right incentive mechanism but has no supporting infrastructure.** With an average product price of $8–$13, a customer must add 4–7 items to qualify — a reasonable ask for a loyal buyer, an invisible barrier for a first-timer. There is no cart progress bar, no "you're $X away from free shipping" nudge, and no "complete your ritual" bundle that makes hitting the threshold feel natural. The infuser ($5), mug ($8), and tea ($8–$13) together tell a coherent starter-kit story, but they're sold separately with no cross-sell module connecting them. AOV is being left on the table not because the catalog is wrong, but because the cart experience doesn't tell shoppers what to add next.

**The retention and acquisition surface exists but isn't closed.** The blog publishes tea-based mocktail recipes (Sleepy Mocktail, Green Tea Mocktail, Carajillo) that directly feature the brand's SKUs — but with no "shop this recipe" CTA. The homepage organizes products by use case (Sleep, Immune, Energy, Digestion) — but there are no standalone landing pages for those use cases that could rank for health-intent queries like "organic sleep tea" or "tea for immune support." An ambassador program exists. A newsletter subscribe exists. None of these connect to a post-purchase reorder loop. The pieces are there; the mechanical execution is missing.

## Proposed experiments

### exp-r1a2b3c4d5e6 — Add back-in-stock capture to all sold-out PDPs

**Pillar:** Conversion
**Affected surface:** Sold-out product pages (Premium Sencha, Organic Sleep Tea, Unwind, Heartburn)
**URL:** https://zenrojas.com/products/premiumsenchagreentea
**Evidence:** https://zenrojas.com/collections/teas — live crawl confirms 4 of 8 tea SKUs show "Sold Out" with no email capture, waitlist button, or restock ETA; sold-out PDPs currently terminate with no next action
**Hypothesis:** Restock conversion rate improves when sold-out pages capture demand instead of silently bouncing it. Visitors landing on sold-out PDPs have already cleared product discovery and category navigation — they're high-intent buyers being lost permanently. A back-in-stock email captures that intent at near-zero cost and converts at 20–30% when the product returns.
**Primary change:** Add a "Notify me when it's back" email input to all sold-out PDPs above the fold, replacing or supplementing the disabled add-to-cart button. Trigger a single restock email when inventory is updated. Add an estimated restock window ("Restocking soon — be the first to know") to set expectations and increase sign-up rate.
**Primary KPI:** Back-in-stock email capture rate on sold-out PDPs
**Decision rule:** Ship immediately; any capture rate above 0% outperforms the current zero. Measure conversion-to-purchase rate once product restocks.
**Expected lift:** +20–35% restock purchase rate among captured emails
**Confidence:** 88%

### exp-r3k4l5m6n7o8 — Add a cart progress bar to the free-shipping threshold

**Pillar:** AOV
**Affected surface:** Cart drawer / cart page
**URL:** https://zenrojas.com/cart
**Evidence:** https://zenrojas.com/collections/teas and https://zenrojas.com/products/tea-seeper — homepage confirms "FREE SHIPPING ON ALL $50+ ORDERS" banner; live crawl of Tea Infuser ($5) and Bodyguard Tea ($13) PDPs shows no cart nudge, progress bar, or "add X more for free shipping" message; average SKU price $8–$13 means threshold requires 4–7 items without guidance
**Hypothesis:** AOV increases when shoppers can see exactly how far they are from a meaningful threshold. Progress bars toward free shipping are one of the highest-ROI Shopify cart features — they convert passive browsing into active threshold-chasing without requiring a discount.
**Primary change:** Add a cart progress bar (via Shopify cart drawer or a free app like Hextom Free Shipping Bar) showing "You're $X away from free shipping." At $0–$30, show the bar with a product suggestion ("Add a Tea Bag Sampler for $5"). At $30–$49, show "Almost there — add one more item." At $50+, celebrate with "Free shipping unlocked!" Pair with a "You might also like" row of 2–3 in-stock products.
**Primary KPI:** Average order value
**Decision rule:** Ship if AOV increases by ≥$4 without reducing checkout conversion rate.
**Expected lift:** +15–25% AOV
**Confidence:** 80%
