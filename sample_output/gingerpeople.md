## Executive summary

**The Ginger People has a deep proof story and a thin handoff.** Across GIN GINS, Ginger Rescue, and Juices, the brand carries some of the strongest functional claims in natural ginger — more fresh ginger than any other brand, 425–800mg ginger-active lozenges, nausea-relief credentials across travel, morning sickness, and GLP-1 side effects — but the path from "I'm interested" to "I bought it" is broken at every junction. The site is retailer-routed by design, a legitimate model, but the retailer handoff is absent where it matters most: the Where To Buy page sits in the primary nav yet carries no store locator, no retailer map, and no online buying cards. Product pages confirm use cases without providing the next step to act on them. For a brand that does not sell direct, weak retailer handoff is the same as a broken checkout.

**The content surface is the brand's biggest underused asset.** The site publishes recipes, a GLP-1/nausea education hub, motion-sickness content, and morning-sickness guidance — a content depth most CPG brands cannot match. None of this content closes to a product recommendation or a retailer action. The GLP-1 nausea space in particular is expanding fast (Medscape, 2026: "Ginger Dietary Supplement May Ease GLP-1-Related Nausea"), and Ginger People carries the right SKUs — Ginger Rescue Extra Strength Lozenges, organic ginger juice shots — but has no structured routine or landing page to capture that intent. Every high-ranking health article is leaving money on the table.

**The catalog breadth creates a discovery tax that nobody is fixing.** GIN GINS alone has 9+ SKUs — Original, Double Strength, Super Strength, Lemon, Mandarin Orange, Peanut, Spicy Apple, Spicy Turmeric, Spice Drops, Sweet Gummies — presented as a flat product list with no flavor guide, heat hierarchy, or trial entry point. First-time buyers face decision paralysis on the category page. The three fixes that move the needle fastest: (1) build a real retailer handoff on Where To Buy, (2) add a buying action module to every product page, and (3) launch a flavor finder and sampler pack for the candy catalog.
